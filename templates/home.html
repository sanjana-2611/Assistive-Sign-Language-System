Why this project matters

Communication barriers isolate many deaf and hard‑of‑hearing individuals from everyday interactions. Most people do not understand sign language, which makes even simple tasks—asking for help, ordering food, or joining a conversation—unnecessarily difficult.

This system acts as a quiet interpreter, turning hand gestures into readable and speakable language, so both sides can focus on what matters: the message, not the barrier.

How it works

  • Multi‑model gesture detection

    Three specialized YOLOv11 models track letters and different word vocabularies to robustly recognize a wide range of hand signs.

  • Semantic sentence building

    Instead of outputting broken word‑by‑word translations, an LLM rearranges the recognized tokens into fluent, grammatically correct sentences.

  • Coaching‑friendly feedback

    Visual overlays and text feedback help learners see exactly how their signs are interpreted, reinforcing correct gestures.
